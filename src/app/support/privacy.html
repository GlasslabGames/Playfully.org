Children’s Privacy Policy

We take great pride in our relationship with you. As such, we are dedicated to protecting your privacy and handling any personal information we obtain from you with care and respect. This Children’s Privacy Policy is designed to answer your questions regarding our privacy policies and principles with respect to children under the age of 13 (each a “child” and collectively, “children.”)

Building on our general Privacy Policy, we recognize the need to provide additional privacy protections when children visit the sites on which this Children’s Privacy Policy is posted (“Sites”). We explain those additional protections here in this Children’s Privacy Policy. For your convenience, this Children’s Privacy Policy uses terms that are defined in our general Privacy Policy.

The Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (“COPPA”) requires that we inform parents and legal guardians about how we collect, use, and disclose personal information from children under 13 years of age; that we obtain the consent of parents and guardians in order for children under 13 years of age to use certain features of our web sites; and that we provide parents access to their child’s personal information to review and/or have the information deleted. Below we explain how we do this for children. Also, when we use the term “parent” below, we mean to include legal guardians.

Our Site provides two ways for children to access and use the products and services made available on, by or through our Site, including all games and learning tools (“Services”). The first is when the parent registers for the Services for use by their child(ren). The second is when a teacher registers for the Services for use by their students. When we use the term “teacher” below we mean to also include administrators of the school, district or classroom. If you are not of legal age to form a binding contract (in many jurisdictions, this age is 18), you may only use the Services and disclose information to us with your parent’s express consent. Please review this Children’s Privacy Policy, our general Privacy Policy and our Terms and Conditions Agreement with your parent.

  1. What types of personal information do we collect about children?

    In order for a child to use our Services, the parent or teacher must register the child and obtain a Class Code. In the registration process, the parent or teacher will be asked to provide information about him or herself, including the parent’s or teacher’s name, email address, grade level(s) of the child(ren)/students, and the name of the institution (school or district) where the teacher works. Children do not provide or disclose any personal information on our Site. In certain cases, we also may collect a child’s e-mail address from a parent or teacher for the purpose of sending the child e-mail related to his/her registration and for customer support. Providing a child’s email is optional. We may collect additional personal information from parents and teachers in order to allow the child to participate in certain features located on our Site.

  2. How do we use and share the personally identifiable information we have collected about children?

    Personal information regarding a child may be collected and used to enable a child’s registration for the Services, to personalize communications to a child regarding a specific game, learning tool or newsletter. Their personal information may also be used for assessment purposes in the game or learning tool and will not be shared with any third parties except for the group or school under which they registered. Some interactive activities may involve disclosure of a child’s personal information to third parties and our partners and operators. In addition, we may share a child’s personal information with third parties to the extent reasonabl necessary to: protect the security of integrity of our sites; to take precautions against liability; to respond to judicial process; or to the extent permitted under provisions of law, to provide information to law enforcement agencies or for an investigation related to public safety.

    Prior to disclosing any personal information regarding a child with any third party operators or service providers, we receive representations from them that their privacy policies are COPPA compliant. We periodically request re-verification of this.

    If you are a child who is signed up for the Services and has a Class Code, you understand that your parent and/or teacher can view all information and activity within or associated with your account, including, without limitation, all personal information, any messages between you and your teacher and grades and assessment you receive on projects.

  3. How do we notify and obtain consent from parents for the collection of information from their children?

    We only collect personal information from a child where that child’s parent or teacher has agreed to the terms of Services via the End User License Agreement and (a) in the case of a parent, has provided consent for the child to use the Services and to provide personal information to us. To use and access our Services, the parent or teacher must first register the child and obtain a Class Code for their child or students. Childen are not allowed to self-register or initiate the registration process or to access the Services prior to the parent’s registration of his/her account and consent to our Site’s Terms and Conditions Agreement. Once the parent/teacher is registered, the parent or teacher will receive a Class Code and with that code the child will be able to use the Services including viewing content, playing games, using our learning tools and participating in the learning activities for which their Class Code applies.

  4. How can parents access, change, or delete personally identifiable information about their children?

    At any time a parent can refuse to permit us to collect further personal information from their child and can request that any personal information we have collected be deleted from our records. We use two methods to allow parents to access, change, or delete the personally identifiable information that we have collected from their child. Depending upon what a parent requests, it may be necessary to delete a child’s registration completely.

    A parent can access, change or delete personal information from their child and can request that any personal information we have collected be deleted from our records. We use two methods to allow parents to access, change, or delete the personally identifiable information that we have collected from their child. Depending upon what a parent requests, it may be necessary to delete a child’s registration completely.

    A parent can contact our Member Services department to access, change, or delete the personal information we have collected from his or her child by sending an e-mail to support@glasslabgames.org. We ask that you please include the child’s member name and the parent’s e-mail address in the requesting email so that we can better assist you with your inquiry or request.

  5. How will be notify parents if our Children’s Privacy Policy Changes?

    We may amend our Children’s Privacy Policy at any time. We will provide parents with notice, using the e-mail currently listed as a parent email address, of any material changes in the way we intend to collect, use, and/or share children’s personal information. Please note that, at all times, parents should update their personal information to provide us current e-mail addresses. We will apply material changes in our Children’s Privacy Policy in conformance with applicable law, including any applicable provisions of COPPA policies that require parental consent.
